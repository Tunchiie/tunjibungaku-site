üìä Project Overview

Comprehensive analysis of over 130,000 Billboard Hot 100 entries cross-referenced with Spotify's Exportify data to discover patterns in chart-topping audio features. This project applies advanced statistical methodologies to isolate key drivers of hit probability and uncover temporal trends in music preferences.

üéØ Key Achievements

  • Large-Scale Data Analysis: Processed and analyzed over 130,000 Billboard Hot 100 entries with corresponding audio features
  • Statistical Validation: Applied rigorous statistical tests including ANOVA, Tukey HSD, and t-tests for robust analysis
  • Seasonal Pattern Discovery: Identified significant seasonal variations - winter tracks consistently trend quieter across decades
  • Temporal Trend Analysis: Documented decade-based shifts showing modern hits skew louder and less acoustic
  • Advanced Visualizations: Created comprehensive data visualizations including heatmaps, violin plots, and correlation matrices
  • Industry Applications: Delivered actionable insights for music producers and playlist engineers

üìà Project Impact & Scale

üî¨ Statistical Methodology

ANOVA Testing
Analysis of Variance to identify statistically significant differences in audio features between hit and non-hit tracks across multiple categorical variables.
Tukey HSD Post-hoc
Honest Significant Difference testing for pairwise comparisons to isolate specific feature differences while controlling family-wise error rate.
T-Test Analysis
Independent samples t-tests to validate mean differences in continuous audio features between successful and unsuccessful tracks.
Mutual Information
Non-parametric method to measure dependencies between audio features and chart success, capturing non-linear relationships.
